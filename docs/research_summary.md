# Research Summary

## Topic

Infodemic spreading, misinformation, and fact-checking strategies in social networks.

## Research Goal

The goal of this project is to study how misinformation-like content spreads through network structures and how different fact-checking strategies can reduce large-scale infodemic outbreaks.

## Main Idea

The project models misinformation and fact-checking as two competing spreading processes on a graph.

Users can be in one of three states:

- `S` — susceptible;
- `F` — exposed to claim-like or fake-like content;
- `C` — exposed to fact-checking content.

## Project Parts

1. Synthetic infodemic percolation model.
2. Empirical validation on the Hoaxy dataset.

## Key Findings

- Infodemic spreading can be interpreted as a percolation-like process.
- Echo chambers strengthen local spread.
- Bridge links help content move between communities.
- Central nodes and influencers play a disproportionate role.
- Targeted fact-checking is more effective than random intervention.
- Fact-checking delay reduces intervention effectiveness.
